Privacy Policy

Effective: March 6, 2026

Overview

Puster ("the app") is a breathing rate indicator for infants. Your privacy is important to us. This policy explains what data the app accesses and how it is handled.

Data Collection

Puster does not collect, store, or transmit any personal data. The app does not have user accounts, analytics, or tracking of any kind.

Microphone Access

Puster uses the device microphone to listen for breathing sounds during measurement sessions. All audio is processed entirely on-device in real time. No audio is recorded, stored, or transmitted.

Measurement Data

Measurement results (breathing rate, confidence, timestamp) are stored locally on your device using on-device storage. This data never leaves your device unless you use the optional AI analysis feature.

Optional AI Analysis

If you choose to enable AI analysis by providing your own API key in Settings, measurement summary data (not audio) is sent to the AI provider you configure (e.g., OpenRouter, OpenAI, Anthropic). This is entirely optional and requires your explicit configuration. Puster does not provide API keys or accounts for these services.

Third-Party Services

The app does not integrate any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs.

Children's Privacy

While Puster is designed to help monitor infants' breathing rates, the app is intended for use by parents and caregivers, not children. The app does not collect any data from children.

Changes

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page.
